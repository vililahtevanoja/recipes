# Brown Butter Skillet Cookie

## Ingredients

- `180 g` unsalted butter, divided
- `135 g`  (~`1.8 dl`) packed dark brown sugar
- `100 g` (~`1.2 dl`) granulated sugar
- `2 tsp` vanilla extract
- `1 tsp` salt
- `1` large egg
- `1` large egg yolk
- `210 g` (~`4.2 dl`) all-purpose flour
- `0.5 tsp` baking soda
- `170 g` semi-sweet chocolate chips or chunks
- Optional: a couple handfuls of crushed salted pretzels

## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to upper-middle position. Preheat oven to `190 °C`.
1. Melt `135 g` butter in a `30 cm` cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Continue to cook, stirring constantly, for 5 minutes or until butter is dark golden brown, has a nutty aroma, and bubbling subsides. Transfer to a large bowl.
1. Stir the remaining `45 g` butter into the hot butter until completely melted. Whisk sugars, vanilla, and salt into melted butter until smooth. Whisk in egg and yolk until smooth, about 30 seconds. Let mixture stand for 3 minutes, then whisk for 30 seconds. Repeat the resting and whisking process 2 more times until mixture is thick, smooth, and shiny.
1. Whisk flour and baking soda together in a separate bowl, then stir flour mixture into butter mixture until just combined, about 1 minute. Stir in chocolate chips, making sure no flour pockets remain.
1. Wipe skillet clean with paper towels. Transfer dough to skillet and press into an even layer with a spatula.
1. Transfer skillet to oven and bake 20 minutes, or until cookie is golden brown and edges are set, rotating skillet halfway through baking. Transfer skillet to a wire rack and let cookie cool for 30 minutes. Slice cookie into wedges and serve.
